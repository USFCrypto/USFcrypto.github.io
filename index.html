Mission

Research, training, and outreach in modern cryptography

The USF Cryptography Center brings together faculty, students, and collaborators working across post-quantum cryptography, coding theory, quantum information, and applied cryptography. Through research programs, seminars, and outreach initiatives, the center supports both foundational work and the training of future researchers.

Research

Research areas

Post-Quantum Cryptography

Lattices, codes, isogenies, and the mathematical foundations of next-generation cryptography.

Coding Theory

Error-correcting codes, code-based cryptography, and algebraic methods for reliable computation and communication.

Quantum Information

Quantum algorithms, cryptographic implications of quantum computation, and mathematically grounded quantum research.

Applied Cryptography

Protocol design, secure systems, privacy-enhancing technology, and implementation-driven cryptographic research.
