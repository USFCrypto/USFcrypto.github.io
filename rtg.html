Overview

Building a durable Applied Algebra ecosystem

The NSF Research Training Group in Applied Algebra at the University of South Florida is designed to strengthen research, training, and community-building at the interface of algebra and modern information technology. The RTG focuses on three tightly connected areas — cryptography, coding theory, and quantum computing — while creating a research culture in which students advance through sustained mentoring rather than isolated experiences.

Instead of separating trainees by academic level, the RTG promotes vertically integrated research teams in which undergraduates, graduate students, postdoctoral associates, and faculty learn together, collaborate on common mathematical problems, and develop the professional habits needed for long-term success in research, teaching, and leadership.

Research pillars

Three areas, one mathematical conversation

The RTG is organized around three pillars whose interactions create strong opportunities for cross-pollination, collaborative mentoring, and mathematically rich applications.

C

Cryptography

Research on hard algebraic and number-theoretic problems that support next-generation cryptography, including quantum-resistant methods and the mathematical foundations of secure communication.

CT

Coding Theory

Mathematical tools for error correction, storage reliability, and code-based cryptography, with attention to both foundational structure and modern computing applications.

Q

Quantum Computing

Quantum algorithms for algebraic problems, mathematically informed circuit design, and interactions between quantum information, error correction, and computational algebra.

Training model

A pathway from first research experience to research leadership

The RTG is built as a layered ecosystem in which each stage supports the next, making mentoring an essential part of the research culture rather than a side activity.

Undergraduates

Entry into research

Students are introduced to active problems through academic-year opportunities, summer research, certificate pathways, seminars, and close mentoring.

Graduate students

Research depth

Graduate trainees develop technical expertise, collaborate across the RTG, and gain experience in communication, teaching, and project development.

Postdocs

Research leadership

Postdoctoral associates help drive projects forward, mentor junior researchers, and grow into independent scholars with broad professional portfolios.

Faculty network

Collaborative mentoring

Faculty at USF and partner institutions provide mathematical guidance, interdisciplinary connections, and continuity across cohorts and programs.

Signature activities

Research, mentoring, and professional growth throughout the year

The RTG combines research activity with intentional professional development, communication training, and community-building across academic levels.

Vertically integrated teams

Collaborative research teams connect postdocs, graduate students, undergraduates, and faculty around shared mathematical problems and regular interaction.

Multi-tier mentoring

Layered mentoring structures support both technical growth and belonging, while helping trainees learn how to mentor others in turn.

Applied Algebra seminar

A recurring seminar creates common intellectual ground for the RTG community and connects local activity with the broader research landscape.

Workshops and roundtables

Dedicated events strengthen mathematical communication, career awareness, research presentation skills, and broader professional preparation.

Peer tutoring and cross-cohort support

Students learn within a culture of mutual assistance, reinforcing both mastery and community across different stages of the training pipeline.

Connections to existing initiatives

The RTG builds on USF strengths including the REU site, quantum initiative, undergraduate pathways, postdoctoral activity, and outreach efforts.

Florida Hub

Extending Applied Algebra beyond one department

A distinctive part of the RTG vision is the creation of a Florida Hub for Applied Algebra — a broader network designed to connect mathematicians and collaborators across the state, including faculty and students at partner institutions and minority-serving institutions.

The goal is not only to strengthen research ties, but also to share mentoring models, training infrastructure, and best practices for recruiting and supporting the next generation of researchers in strategic mathematical areas.

Statewide collaboration

Build sustained ties among researchers in algebra, cryptography, coding theory, and quantum information.

Cross-institution mentoring

Create new bridges between students, faculty, and emerging researchers across Florida institutions.

Dissemination of best practices

Share effective approaches to retention, mentoring, and high-quality research preparation.
